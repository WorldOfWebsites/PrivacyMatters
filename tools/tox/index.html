What is Tox?

Tox is a peer-to-peer, end to end encrypted communication system, supporting text, audio and video calling. There are no servers to deal with as the client makes a direct connection to the other user(s). Each person is given a Tox ID, which is your way of contacting someone.

Tox on its own refers to the protocol, built on proven cryptographic protocols. Experts say that it is promising, but this is still experimental, so use at your own risk.

There are many different clients for Tox, for Windows, OSX, Linux, Android and IOS. These include qTox, the more lightweight cousin uTox, Toxic, which lives in the command line on Linux, and more.

It is important to know that this does not provide anonymity, which is the nature of all peer-to-peer programs. In order for them to work, IP addresses have to be exchanged. You can use a proxy, which you can configure on at least some of the clients, or tunnel all your devices connections through a proxy, VPN or Tor.
