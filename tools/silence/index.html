Silence is a fork of TextSecure (now Signal) that retains encrypted SMS/MMS. The reason why SMS/MMS encryption was removed can be found here. Please read this, since there are some valid concerns. For example, metadata will be sent unencrypted and to your carrier, allowing the government and others to access them.

The app will encrypt messages on the device if you have a passphrase enabled, and over the air if you are communicating with another Silence user. The lock at the top of a conversation will allow you to exchange keys. Only until you have both exchanged keys, will you be able to chat with end-to-end encryption.

Due to restrictions on IOS, Silence is only available on Android. Furthermore, that means that encrypted SMS/MMS conversations will not be possible between IOS and Android devices. If that is something you need, use Signal.
